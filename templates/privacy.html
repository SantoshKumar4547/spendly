Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 8, 2026

Your privacy matters. This Privacy Policy explains what information Spendly collects, how we use it, and the choices you have. By using Spendly, you agree to the practices described below.

1. Data We Collect

When you create an account, we collect basic account information such as your name, email address, and a hashed password. When you use Spendly, we store the expense entries, categories, dates, amounts, and any notes or descriptions you choose to add.

We also collect limited technical information — such as your IP address, browser type, and device identifiers — to keep your account secure, prevent abuse, and diagnose service issues.

2. How We Use Your Data

We use the information you provide to operate Spendly's core features: logging expenses, generating summaries and category breakdowns, and showing your history across sessions and devices. Account information is used to authenticate you and to communicate important service notices.

We do not sell your personal data. We do not use the contents of your expense records for advertising, profiling, or any commercial purpose unrelated to operating the service.

3. Data Storage and Security

Your data is stored on secure servers and protected with industry standard safeguards, including encrypted connections (HTTPS) in transit and access controls at rest. Passwords are stored as cryptographic hashes; no employee or contractor has routine access to your raw expense entries.

While we work hard to protect your information, no system is perfectly secure. You are responsible for keeping your account credentials confidential and for using a strong, unique password.

4. Third-Party Services

Spendly relies on a small set of trusted third parties to operate — for example, a database or cloud hosting provider, and an email service for account notifications. These providers only receive the information needed to perform their function and are bound by confidentiality and security obligations.

We do not embed third-party advertising trackers, analytics pixels, or social media scripts in Spendly. If this changes in the future, we will update this policy and notify you before it takes effect.

5. Your Choices and Rights

You can review, update, or delete the data in your account at any time through your account settings. You may also request a full export of your data or close your account; doing so will permanently delete the personal information we hold about you, except where we are required to retain certain records for legal or accounting purposes.

6. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy to reflect changes in our service, legal requirements, or business practices. When we make material changes, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page and, where appropriate, notify you through the service.

7. Contact Us

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy, or wish to exercise your data rights, please reach out to us through the support channels listed in the app.
